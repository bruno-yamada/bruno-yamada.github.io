SITE RELIABILITY ENGINEER - Reading Notes

Chapter 1 - Introduction

Hope is not a strategy

At Google, most SREs are either from SoftDev team or are from closely related positions, but almost always they are devs

Google’s approach has SREs dedicate at max 50% of their time to ops tickets, the other 50% is spent in developing

We want systems that are automatic, not just automated

The SRE team is responsible for: availability, latency, performance, efficiency, change management, monitoring, emergency response, and capacity planning of their service

100% SLA is unreasonable, since, for web systems, the difference between 99.999% and 100% is mostly unnoticeable by the user, since their laptop,wifi,ISP,powergrid and such are collective also responsible for the SLA perceived by the user, thus 100% is not worth, since that last 0.001% is not worth the effort. So you can assume something like 99.9% SLA, and them you have an “error budget” of 0.1%, now, with this error budget, and you can consider it as a budget, you have some margin to spend that budget with as the strategy sees fit. If the development team wans to deploy as quickly as possible, and the SREs wants to keep the system up as much as possible, with an budget of 0.1%, you can make some margin for minor errors, as long as you dont overspend your budget

Monitoring

Paramound for SREs, but it need to be taken right, there are 3 kinds of monitoring outputs: - Alerts: demands human action immediately, in response to something that happened or is about to happen - Tickets: human needs to take action, but not immeadiately, it is affecting the system, but action can be taken in a few days without causing damage to the system - Logging: No one needs to look at this information, but it is recorded for diagnostic or forensic purposes

Emergency response

That are 2 metrics, MTTF(mean time to failure) and MTTR(mean time to repair), most important being how long the team can put a system back online MTTR Humans add latency to MTTR, so automatic responses area always faster, an ninja dev can think on the fly, but a regular dev with a proper playbooks(step-by-step recovery plans) can work faster than him.

Change Management

At google, 70% of problems are due to code change to live system, as such its best to: - progressive rollout - quickly detect problem - rollback safely when problem occur

Demand Forecasting and Capacity Planning

Try to forecast demand, organic or not, have to proper capacity to proccess it, do regular load testings to correlate raw capacity(server, disk, network, etc) with service capacity

Efficiency and Performance

SREs predict demand, provision capacity, and can modify the software(they can modify software in some situations) SREs need to monitor and improve efficiency, if the company cares about the money
